Logging In

Logging into your WordPress website.

To login to your WordPress website, go to https://example.com/wp-admin and enter your username and password.

Replace example.com with your website URL.

If you cannot remember your username, use your email instead.

If you can't remember your password, click the Lost your password? link.

WordPress Dashboard

Using your WordPress Dashboard

After logging in, you will be brought to your WordPress dashboard. This is where you will control all of your websites content. The lefthand sidebar allows you to navigate to your posts, pages, settings, and more.

To learn more about how to customize your WordPress dashboard view, check out the WordPress docs.

Learn more about the WordPress Dashboard

Posts

Creating & Managing WordPress Posts

Posts in WordPress are short for blog post or news articles. Think of them as "Blog Posts" even if you don't have a blog. If you have a news section, you'll probably be using "Posts" to manage your content.

To add a new post, click on "Posts" and then click "Add New". Follow the on screen instructions and add your new post. When you're ready to publish your post, click publish.

Think of the main post editor as a Word document. You can bold text, add lists, add headings etc. It's all up to you!
You can add images to your post by using the Media button above the main editor. This will allow you to easily upload and insert images.

To learn more about WordPress posts, check out the WordPress docs on writing posts.

Learn more about writing WordPress posts

Pages

Creating & Managing WordPress Pages

Pages in WordPress are static and traditional pieces of website content. Pages are static and are not listed by date. Pages do not use tags or categories, like a blog post would. An About page is the classic example. Pages are often displayed in the main navigation of the site.

To add a new page, click on "Page" and then click "Add New". Follow the on screen instructions and add your new page. When you're ready to publish your page, click publish.

Remember, just like posts, pages have a rich content editor that allows all kinds of styles and media. Think of it as a Word document when adding your content.
Confused about the difference between posts and pages? No problem, check out the link below and read the WordPress pages intro for more details.

Menus

Creating & Managing WordPress Menus

Menus in WordPress refer to your website navigation links. Typically menus are found at the top of a website and help users find the content they're looking for.

Editing or adding to a menu is easy. First go to Appearance ⇢ Menus. To add an item to the menu, select the page you want to add from the left hand side and click Add to Menu.

Menu items can be re-order by simply dragging and dropping the menu item!
Don't forget to click Save Menu after making changes to your menu items.

Settings

Managing WordPress Settings
Your website will have two main settings areas. One for the settings of your actual website (users, administration, privacy settings, etc). The other will be global theme settings specific to the look and feel of your website. Both are covered below.
Website Settings

This Settings General Screen is the default Screen in the Settings Administration Screen and controls some of the most basic configuration settings for your site: your site's title and location, who may register an account at your site, and how dates and times are calculated and displayed.

You can find this area by going to the left hand sidebar and clicking Settings.

You ikely won't need to change any items here. Be careful of the settings you change (such as the website URL) as these changes may alter your website functionality. When in doubt, just email us. We're happy to help.
Learn more about the general WordPress settings
Theme Settings

Global theme settings can be found in the bottom left hand sidebar. These setitngs relate to the actual content and visuals of your theme. This area is used to control content/designs that appear throughout the site (not limited to one page). A good example is the footer text of your website.

Theme settings are tailored specifically for you. They will have instructions and descriptions above each setting to make life easy!
